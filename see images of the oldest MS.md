### 👉 See images of the oldest manuscript and its transcription

The diplomatic transcription of the oldest surviving manuscript, [MS Kathmandu Kaiser Library 699](https://panditproject.org/entity/102306/manuscript), with facing manuscript images:

  1. [Sūtrasthāna](https://sushrutaproject1.github.io/Sushrutasamhita_Saktumiva1/xml/01-su.su/kl_699_sutrasthana.xml)
  2. [Nidānasthāna](https://sushrutaproject1.github.io/Sushrutasamhita_Saktumiva1/xml/02-su.ni/kl_699_nidanasthana.xml). Missing from KL 699 except for part of the last adhyāya.
  3. Śārīrasthāna. Missing from KL 699.
  4. [Cikitsāsthāna](https://sushrutaproject1.github.io/Sushrutasamhita_Saktumiva1/xml/04-su.ci/kl_699_cikitsasthana.xml). Missing from KL 699 up to adhyāya 18.
  5. [Kalpasthāna](https://sushrutaproject1.github.io/Sushrutasamhita_Saktumiva1/xml/05-su.ka/kl_699_kalpasthana.xml).
  6. [Uttaratantra](https://sushrutaproject1.github.io/Sushrutasamhita_Saktumiva1/xml/06-su.ut/kl_699_uttaratantra.xml).
